The Pinnacle of the Valley

Luxury Without
Compromise

Indian Wells is the Coachella Valley's crown jewel — an incorporated city of fewer than 5,000 residents that consistently ranks among America's wealthiest communities per capita. The city subsidizes luxury amenities for its residents at every turn: discounted rounds at the acclaimed Indian Wells Golf Resort, the IW Club dining experience, and access to resort facilities that most cities could only dream of providing.

Home to the BNP Paribas Open — one of the most prestigious tennis tournaments outside of the Grand Slams — the Indian Wells Tennis Garden brings the world's best players to this quiet desert city every March. It's a moment that defines Indian Wells: world-class excellence in a private, intimate setting.

Properties here range from custom estate homes on oversized lots to elegant condominiums within guarded country club enclaves. Every home in Indian Wells is a significant purchase — and with Shana's expertise, you'll make it confidently.

Living in

BNP Paribas Open

Every March, Indian Wells hosts the world's top tennis players in a tournament that rivals the Grand Slams in prestige. Residents get preferred access — and walk to the matches.

Communities like Desert Horizons, Eldorado, and Indian Wells Country Club offer the ultimate in privacy, security, and prestige — with HOA amenities that rival five-star resorts.

Indian Wells residents receive exclusive discounts at the Hyatt Regency, Miramonte, and Renaissance Esmeralda — dining, spa, and pool access at below-market rates.

Top Investment Performance

Indian Wells home values have consistently outpaced the valley average. Scarcity of supply, demand from high-net-worth buyers, and city amenities make this a resilient luxury market.

The Santa Rosa and San Jacinto Mountains create a dramatic three-sided backdrop. Nearly every property in Indian Wells enjoys dramatic, protected mountain sightlines.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About Indian Wells

The questions buyers ask most when exploring Indian Wells real estate.

What is the median home price in Indian Wells?
+
Indian Wells has the highest median home price in the Coachella Valley at approximately $1.2M–$1.5M as of 2026. The city's small size (approximately 5,000 residents), ultra-luxury guard-gated communities, and extremely limited inventory keep values elevated. Entry points start around $600K–$800K in smaller condominium communities, while premier estates at The Reserve or Vintage Club frequently trade above $5M.
What makes Indian Wells unique among Coachella Valley cities?
+
Indian Wells holds the distinction of having the highest per-capita income of any city in California. Its five guard-gated communities — The Reserve, Vintage Club, Indian Wells Country Club, Eldorado Country Club, and Desert Horizons — offer unparalleled exclusivity and privacy. City residents receive significant discounts on golf at the Indian Wells Golf Resort and on tickets to the BNP Paribas Open, making the city lifestyle a genuine benefit of ownership.
Who is the typical Indian Wells buyer?
+
Indian Wells attracts high-net-worth individuals seeking maximum privacy, prestige, and world-class amenities. Buyers include CEOs, entertainers, professional athletes, and retirees from major metros (LA, San Francisco, New York, Chicago). Most properties serve as vacation or second homes, though a growing segment of buyers are becoming full-time residents attracted by quality of life, healthcare access, and California tax considerations.
What golf clubs are in Indian Wells?
+
The Indian Wells Golf Resort offers two public championship courses (Players Course and Celebrity Course) with significant discounts for city residents. Private club options include Vintage Club (considered one of the top 10 private clubs in America), The Reserve Club, Eldorado Country Club, and Desert Horizons Country Club. Membership in these private clubs is highly selective and often a separate significant investment beyond the home purchase.
Is Indian Wells a good investment market?
+
Indian Wells is one of the most stable appreciation markets in the valley due to its extremely limited supply, prestige barriers to entry, and consistent demand from affluent buyers. However, it is not typically a short-term rental market — most communities prohibit STRs entirely, and the buyer profile skews toward primary luxury and vacation use rather than income production. Shana can help you understand the long-term investment case for any specific property.
How does the BNP Paribas Open affect Indian Wells real estate?
+
The BNP Paribas Open — one of the largest tennis tournaments outside the four Grand Slams — brings 450,000+ visitors annually to Indian Wells Tennis Garden, generating exceptional short-term rental premiums in nearby cities. Indian Wells itself sees limited STR activity due to HOA restrictions, but the tournament's global profile significantly elevates the city's brand recognition and supports sustained demand from international buyers who first discover the valley through the event.
