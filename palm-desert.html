The Valley's Cultural Hub

Shopping, Arts &
Desert Elegance

Palm Desert sits at the heart of the Coachella Valley and has long been the preferred address for those who want it all — sophisticated shopping on the famous El Paseo "Rodeo Drive of the Desert," a thriving arts community, premier dining, and easy access to world-class golf.

From guard-gated luxury communities like The Reserve, Bighorn, and Desert Falls to charming single-family neighborhoods and active-adult enclaves, Palm Desert offers an extraordinary range of housing to match every lifestyle. It's also home to College of the Desert and the McCallum Theatre, making it one of the valley's most complete cities.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About Palm Desert

The questions buyers ask most when exploring Palm Desert real estate.

What is the median home price in Palm Desert?
+
Median home price is approximately $550,000–$600,000 in Palm Desert as of 2026. The market offers wide range — from affordable condos starting near $300K in communities like Monterey Country Club to luxury estates on the country club corridor exceeding $3M. El Paseo and south Palm Desert generally command premium pricing.
What makes El Paseo significant for Palm Desert real estate?
+
El Paseo is known as the "Rodeo Drive of the Desert" — a mile-long luxury shopping and dining corridor that anchors Palm Desert's identity and drives significant foot traffic, tourism, and business investment. Properties within walking distance of El Paseo carry a premium, and the corridor's continued retail investment supports long-term value for nearby homeowners.
Is Palm Desert good for primary residences vs. vacation homes?
+
Palm Desert is one of the strongest markets for both. As the valley's commercial and services hub (College of the Desert, major medical centers, the largest retail concentration in the CV), it appeals strongly to full-time residents. At the same time, its 350 days of sunshine, resort lifestyle, and proximity to Palm Springs make it equally popular for snowbirds and vacation home buyers. HOA restrictions on short-term rentals vary by community — always verify before purchasing for investment.
What country clubs and golf communities exist in Palm Desert?
+
Palm Desert is home to dozens of golf and country club communities, including Bighorn Golf Club (ultra-luxury, members-only), Desert Falls Country Club, Ironwood Country Club, Monterey Country Club, Palm Desert Country Club, Chaparral Country Club, and many more. Each has different HOA structures, fee levels, and ownership requirements. Shana can walk you through the club membership options as part of your home search.
What are the best neighborhoods in Palm Desert?
+
South Palm Desert (near El Paseo and Indian Wells border) is the most prestigious area, with large custom estates. The country club corridor along Country Club Drive offers resort-style living at a range of price points. North Palm Desert near Cook Street is more affordable and appeals to first-time buyers. College of the Desert area is seeing active new construction.
How does the College of the Desert affect the Palm Desert market?
+
College of the Desert (COD) brings 28,000+ students annually and anchors the city's education, workforce, and cultural programming. COD also operates the popular Palm Desert Certified Farmers Market on Wednesdays and Saturdays — a community institution. The college's presence supports property values, drives local spending, and contributes to Palm Desert's year-round vitality beyond just tourist season.
